# Dao Primer

A structured introduction to Daoism — the Chinese tradition of thought and practice built around the Dao (the Way) as the source and animating pattern of all things. The collection moves from the foundational concept through classical texts, organized schools, cosmological figures, and cultivation practices spanning more than two millennia of continuous development.

Audience: the curious newcomer, the student of Chinese philosophy or religion, or anyone who has encountered the Daodejing, Taijiquan, or Chinese medicine and wants the underlying framework laid out plainly.

This is an educational primer, not a guide to practice or a translation of primary sources. **The text was generated with AI assistance** as a synthesis of scholarly material and edited for consistency. Treat it as a study aid, not an authoritative source.

## Jing, Qi, and Shen

Daoist cosmology describes the world as [Qi](Qi.md) condensing from undifferentiated emptiness into progressively denser forms. Jing (essence), qi (breath), and shen (spirit) mark three stages of that descent. Cultivation practice reverses the direction, refining each substance back into the one above it until the practitioner returns to the Dao's original state.

| Treasure       | Chinese | What It Names                             | Bodily Form                                         |
| -------------- | ------- | ----------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Jing - essence | 精      | Densest; the coagulated final stage of Qi | Reproductive fluids; constitutional vitality        |
| Qi - breath    | 氣      | Animating energy; the middle stage        | Respiration; the force circulating through the body |
| Shen - spirit  | 神      | Most subtle; closest to the Dao           | Cognition, awareness, presence                      |

The three also go by the name _sanbao_ (三寶, "three treasures") in cultivation contexts. The same term applies separately to the three ethical principles named in [Daodejing](Daodejing.md) chapter 67 — compassion (_ci_), frugality (_jian_), and not daring to be first — covered in [The Three Treasures](ThreeTreasures.md). Both uses are standard; context distinguishes them.

[Neidan](Neidan.md) is the classical tradition of inner alchemy that worked this triad most systematically. [Qigong](Qigong.md), [Taijiquan](Taijiquan.md), and [Daoist Meditation](Meditation.md) engage the same substances through different methods.

## Working Vocabulary

Key terms across all pages, with links to their canonical homes.

**Foundational Concepts** - the conceptual bedrock:

- **[Dao](Dao.md)** - the Way; the ineffable source and pattern underlying all things
- **[De](De.md)** - virtue or power; the Dao's presence in a particular thing; each thing's inherent nature
- **[Wu Wei](WuWei.md)** - non-action; movement without forcing, imposition, or manipulation
- **[Ziran](Ziran.md)** - naturalness; "self-so"; the mode in which the Dao generates without commanding
- **[Pu](Pu.md)** - the uncarved block; undifferentiated potential before preference and habit narrow it

**Cosmological Framework** - the tools for describing how things arise and interact:

- **[Yin-Yang](YinYang.md)** - the fundamental complementary polarity present in all things
- **[Qi](Qi.md)** - vital energy; the animating substance through which the Dao moves
- **[Wu Xing](WuXing.md)** - the Five Phases (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water); a system of dynamic correspondences
- **[Jing, Qi, Shen](JingQiShen.md)** - essence, breath, and spirit; the three substances of inner cultivation

**Texts & Canon:**

- **[Daodejing](Daodejing.md)** - 81 chapters attributed to Laozi; the most-referenced text in the collection
- **[The Zhuangzi](Zhuangzi.md)** - the second foundational text; philosophical parables and inner cultivation
- **[The Liezi](Liezi.md)** - a later classical text associated with the philosophical current
- **[The Daozang](Daozang.md)** - the Daoist canon; approximately 1,500 texts compiled under the Ming dynasty

**Recurring Terms:**

- **Sanbao** - "three treasures"; applies to two distinct sets: the Jing-Qi-Shen cultivation triad ([JingQiShen](JingQiShen.md)) and Laozi's three ethical principles ([ThreeTreasures](ThreeTreasures.md))
- **Xian** - immortal or perfected; the human who has merged with the Dao; see [Xian](Xian.md)
- **San Qing** - the Three Pure Ones; the supreme Daoist deities corresponding to the three stages of cosmogony; see [SanQing](SanQing.md)
- **San Jiao** - the three teachings (Daoism, Confucianism, Buddhism) as they coexisted and interacted in Chinese civilization; see [SanJiao](SanJiao.md)
- **Neidan / Waidan** - inner alchemy (working with Jing, Qi, Shen in the body) vs. outer alchemy (laboratory practice with minerals and metals); see [Neidan](Neidan.md)
